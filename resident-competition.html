Resident Competition

A long-running tradition supporting trainee scholarship in Brooklyn.

The Brooklyn Psychiatric Society's resident competition highlights scholarly work, mentorship, and presentation opportunities across the borough's psychiatry training community.

Research Clinical Scholarship Mentorship

Competition focus

Resident and trainee scholarship

The competition creates a borough-wide forum for academic, clinical, and quality-improvement work developed by psychiatry trainees.

Presentation experience

Finalists present to faculty, peers, and society members, building confidence and professional visibility.

Recognition

Awards and recognition help showcase emerging psychiatric scholarship across Brooklyn's training programs.

Typical submission categories

Research

Original studies, literature-informed academic work, and data-driven presentations.

Clinical Case

Case reports or case series that highlight diagnostic, therapeutic, or systems learning.

Quality Improvement

Projects focused on safety, workflow, education, or patient-care improvement.

Education

Teaching innovations, curriculum work, or scholarly educational initiatives.

Annual cycle

Call for submissions

Submission guidelines and deadlines are announced through the society each year.

Review process

Faculty and society reviewers evaluate abstracts, presentations, and scholarly merit.

Presentation night

Selected presenters share their work at a society event or academic evening.

Awards and recognition

Winning and finalist work is recognized through the society's communications and events.

Submission checklist

Abstract

Prepare a concise abstract or summary of the work.

Program affiliation

Identify the training program, mentor, and authorship details.

Presentation materials

Be ready to submit slides, poster files, or supporting materials if requested.
