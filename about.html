About & History

A borough-wide professional home for psychiatry since 1949.

The Brooklyn Psychiatric Society is the Kings County District Branch of the American Psychiatric Association, serving psychiatrists, residents, fellows, and medical students across Brooklyn.

Kings County District Branch Affiliate of NYSPA Education, Advocacy, Community

Who we are

Professional home

The society brings Brooklyn psychiatrists together for education, advocacy, professional connection, and public-facing leadership.

Training community

We represent psychiatrists training and practicing across Brooklyn's academic, hospital, ambulatory, and community psychiatry settings.

Public mission

We support patients and families by promoting access to psychiatric care, public education, and trusted referral pathways.

History at a glance

1949

The Brooklyn Psychiatric Society was founded as the Kings County District Branch of organized psychiatry.

Over the decades

The society built a tradition of member meetings, trainee engagement, resident scholarship, and borough-level professional leadership.

Today

BPS continues to convene psychiatrists across Brooklyn for advocacy, educational programming, resident competitions, and member services.

Brooklyn footprint

Residency programs

Brooklyn training representation includes Brookdale University Hospital, Maimonides Medical Center, SUNY Downstate Health Sciences University, and Interfaith Medical Center.

Practice settings

Members span outpatient clinics, hospitals, consultation-liaison psychiatry, child and adolescent care, emergency psychiatry, and community mental health.

Partnerships

The society maintains ties across organized psychiatry, training programs, community partners, and statewide advocacy efforts.
